<objective>
Append a Grafana annotation rule named "Tail-sampling buffer saturated" to `grafana/dashboards/ose-otel-demo.json` that fires whenever the otel-collector's `tail_sampling` processor reports `sampling_traces_on_memory >= 10000` — the configured `num_traces` cap. The annotation gives workshop attendees a visually obvious red tick on every panel of the workshop dashboard whenever the tail-sampling buffer saturates, making the F2-2 buffer canary lesson click without requiring attendees to scroll to the Phase 11 diagnostics row's bonus Panel 5.

Purpose: the deferred follow-up logged at the bottom of `.planning/debug/resolved/tail-sampling-recordpolicy.md` ("Panel 5 buffer-saturation observation (Plan 11-05 / F2-2 canary)") observed that under workshop load (~200 rps steady) the gauge `otelcol_processor_tail_sampling_sampling_traces_on_memory` pins at `num_traces=10000`. When the buffer saturates, traces older than the eviction window are dropped before reaching the composite policy — the workshop's "all errors are kept" promise can quietly violate. Today only Panel 5 surfaces this. The annotation lifts the same signal up to the global timeline so it bleeds across the projector strip (Recent Traces, Service Graph, RED Metrics, Logs).

Scope: the annotation lives INLINE on the workshop dashboard JSON (locked decision #2). NOT a Prometheus alert rule. NOT a separate provisioning manifest. NOT applied to `ose-otel-noc.json` or `ose-otel-infra.json` (locked decision #6).

Output: `grafana/dashboards/ose-otel-demo.json` extended with one new entry inside `annotations.list`.
</objective>
